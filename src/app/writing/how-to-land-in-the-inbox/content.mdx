You can write the best cold email in the world, and it can still earn nothing, because it never reached a human. Deliverability is the invisible layer that decides whether your message lands in the inbox or dies in spam. Most outbound fails here, quietly, and the sender never knows why.

Here's the setup that keeps you landing.

## Protect your real domain

Outreach never goes out on your primary company domain. You use dedicated domains, so if anything goes wrong, your real email reputation stays untouched.

## Prove you are who you say

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are the records that tell inbox providers the mail is genuinely from you. Without them, you look like a spoofer, and you get treated like one.

## Warm up before you scale

A brand-new mailbox that suddenly sends hundreds of emails looks exactly like a spammer. Warm-up builds a sending reputation gradually, so volume later doesn't trip alarms.

## Spread and pace the sending

Volume is split across a pool of inboxes and sent at a human rhythm. No single mailbox does anything that looks automated or aggressive.

## Watch the health, fix early

Bounce rates, spam placement, and mailbox health are monitored continuously. Trouble gets caught and rotated out before it drags down the whole campaign.

<Callout>
Deliverability is invisible until it's the only thing that matters. Get it
right and everything else you built finally has a chance to work.
</Callout>

None of this is glamorous. It's also the difference between outbound that works and outbound that silently doesn't.
